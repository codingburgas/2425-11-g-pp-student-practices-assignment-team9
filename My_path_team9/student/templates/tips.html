Tips


Study Techniques

  • Use the Pomodoro Technique: Study for 25 minutes, then take a 5-minute break.
  • Teach someone else: Explaining concepts out loud helps solidify understanding.
  • Use active recall and spaced repetition for long-term retention.
  • Create mind maps and diagrams to visualize information.
  • Summarize chapters in your own words.

Time Management Tips

  • Set SMART goals (Specific, Measurable, Achievable, Relevant, Time-bound).
  • Use a planner or digital calendar to block study sessions.
  • Prioritize tasks with the Eisenhower Matrix: urgent vs. important.
  • Avoid multitasking — focus on one task at a time for better productivity.
  • Review your goals weekly and adjust as needed.

Getting Help as a Student

  • Talk to your school counselor or psychologist if you're feeling overwhelmed, anxious, or stressed.
  • Reach out to a trusted teacher, mentor, or family member for support.
  • Join student support groups or peer mentoring programs at your school.
  • Utilize mental health resources provided by your school or community.
  • Don't hesitate to ask for help — it's a sign of strength, not weakness.
